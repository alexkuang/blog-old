Garlic Soy Chicken Wings

Apr 17th, 2014

Yes, I’m going to be that Asian who posts a chicken wing recipe involving some combination of garlic, soy, and honey. Next thing you know I’ll be writing book reviews for the Joy Luck Club and acting in sketches about Tiger Moms. Though I suppose the latter would be pretty entertaining…

This isn’t the first chicken wing recipe I’ve posted (note to self: still need to port those old entries over…), but it is a pretty quick and easy one. The only awkward part is that in situations like this, it’s not worth the effort to not make a ton, but then everything takes a while to eat… But I think that’s a pretty good problem to have.

  • 3 lb chicken wings
  • 1 cup vegetable oil
  • ¼ cup honey
  • ¼ cup soy sauce
  • ¼ cup Shaoxing wine
  • 4 cloves garlic
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar
  • Pinch of salt

To prepare, mix all the sauce ingredients together, and toss the chicken in. If you’re feeling really bored, jab them a few times to help the sauce seep in. Mix evenly, then let it sit in the fridge overnight. In my experience, the wings can be kept this way for up to 3 days—I’d vouch for longer, but they’re generally eaten in that time frame.

When ready, pre-heat the oven to 375, and bake for about 30 minutes. Do note that I usually cook in batches, so if the whole 3 lb needs to be done at once, 45 minutes would probably be safer. Give them a quick toss on the stove with some fresh herbs. Scallions are usually a pretty good bet, but I like cilantro too.
